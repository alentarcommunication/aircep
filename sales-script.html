Business Call Conversion Script

A practical, ready-to-use script to open calls professionally, capture the right details and turn every inquiry into a clear next step.

SECTION 1Opening Greeting — the first 10 seconds

Goal: sound professional, put the caller at ease, and take control of the call.

Standard greeting

"Thank you for calling [Business Name], this is [Your Name]. How can I help you today?"

After-hours / busy version

"Thank you for calling [Business Name]. We're currently assisting other customers — may I take your details so the right person can call you back within [timeframe]?"
Do: answer within 3 rings, use your name, smile while speaking (it carries in your voice).
Don't: answer with just "Hello?", or place the caller on hold before greeting them.

SECTION 2Discovery Questions — understand why they called

Ask one question at a time. Let the caller finish before responding.

  1. "May I know your name, please?"
  2. "What can we help you with today?" (their need or problem)
  3. "Is this your first time contacting us, or are you an existing customer?"
  4. "How soon are you looking to get this done?" (urgency)
  5. "How did you hear about us?" (marketing source)
Tip: Repeat key answers back to confirm — "So you're looking for X by next week, correct?" It builds trust and prevents mistakes.

SECTION 3Lead Qualification Fields — capture before the call ends

FieldNotes
Full name
Phone number
Email
Reason for call / service needed
Urgency (today / this week / this month)
Preferred callback or appointment time
How they found us
Next action agreed

SECTION 4Booking & Callback Language

To book an appointment

"I can schedule that for you right now. We have [Option A] or [Option B] available — which works better for you?"
Why two options? Offering a choice of two times converts better than an open "When would you like to come in?"

To arrange a callback

"Our [specialist / team member] can call you back today between [time range]. What's the best number to reach you?"

Confirmation

"Perfect — you're booked for [day, date, time]. You'll receive a confirmation by [SMS/email]. Is there anything else I can help you with?"

SECTION 5Objection-Handling Prompts

"How much does it cost?" (when an exact quote isn't possible)

"Great question — it depends on [factors]. The best way to give you an accurate answer is a quick [consultation/visit]. I can book that for you now — no obligation."

"I need to think about it."

"Of course. May I send you the details by email so you have everything in one place? And would it help if we held a tentative slot for you — you can cancel anytime."

"I was just enquiring."

"Absolutely, happy to help. So I can send you the right information — may I take your name and email?"

"You're more expensive than others."

"I understand price matters. Many of our customers felt the same until they saw [value/result]. Would a quick 15-minute walkthrough help you compare properly?"

SECTION 6Clear Next-Step Checklist — end of every call

A call is only successful when all of these are true:

  • Caller's name and contact details captured
  • Reason for the call understood and noted
  • Urgency identified
  • One concrete next step agreed (booking / callback / information email)
  • Time for the next step confirmed
  • Caller received confirmation (verbal + SMS/email)
  • Summary forwarded to the right person on your team
Golden rule: no call should end with only "Someone will call you back." Always confirm who, when and about what.
